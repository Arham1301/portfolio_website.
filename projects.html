AI-Powered Product Recommender Using Web Scrapping

I've developed a powerful tool for personalized product recommendations, combining the strengths of web scraping and artificial intelligence. Here's how it works: Using Beautiful Soup, I scrape data from Amazon, extracting detailed product information including material composition. This enables the system to have a rich database of products to recommend from. The user interface allows users to input specific prompts, such as "athletic wear socks that absorb sweat quickly." When a user submits a prompt, the tool processes the input and sends it to OpenAI's GPT-3.5-turbo-instruct API. The AI then analyzes the prompt and evaluates the scraped product data to identify the best matches. For example, given the prompt about sweat-absorbing socks, the AI can recognize that materials like "86.5% polyester and 13.5% spandex" are known for their excellent moisture-wicking properties. It then ranks the products accordingly and returns the top five recommendations to the user.

Credit Risk Analysis

I developed and trained a regression model as a tool for financial risk assessment, enabling accurate predictions of loan defaults and aiding institutions in risk management decisions. I demonstrated the practicality of logistic regression in financial risk assessment, emphasizing its interpretability and achieving an accuracy of 82.58% in predicting loan defaults. The implementation involved leveraging essential libraries such as NumPy, pandas, and matplotlib, and focused on key parameters like income, home ownership, employment length, loan intent, grade, and loan amount.
